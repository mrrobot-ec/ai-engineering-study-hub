# Source and access policy

This library is for personal study. A file is included only when its source clearly
authorizes access. Finding a PDF on the public internet, including in a GitHub
repository, is not by itself evidence that the upload is lawful.

## Access classes

1. **Open license** — the author or publisher supplies the work under a stated open
   license. Keep the license and canonical source in the catalog.
2. **Official free-to-read** — the author, publisher, university, standards body, or
   company intentionally provides the complete file or web edition. Keep it for
   personal use and do not assume redistribution rights.
3. **Official companion material** — code, chapter summaries, notebooks, samples, or
   exercises published by the author or publisher. These may accompany a commercial
   book but do not replace the paid text.
4. **Commercial or library access** — store only bibliographic information and legal
   purchase, subscription, preview, or library links. Do not store an unauthorized
   copy.

## Accepted sources

- Author- or publisher-controlled sites and repositories
- University and recognized research-lab sites
- arXiv, OpenReview, conference proceedings, and institutional repositories
- Official company research or engineering sites
- Standards bodies and nonprofit security organizations
- A repository whose owner is the author, publisher, or official project and whose
  relationship to the material is documented

## Excluded sources

- PDF dumps, book collections, torrents, cyberlockers, and shadow libraries
- Random forks or user repositories containing commercial books
- Search-result snippets or mirrors when a canonical source is unavailable
- Files obtained by bypassing a paywall, login, rate limit, DRM, or access control
- A full commercial book reconstructed from previews, snippets, or samples

## Verification requirements

Every downloaded item should have a canonical URL, access class or license, file
size, page count where applicable, and SHA-256 digest in the catalog. PDFs must open
successfully and contain the expected title/content. Broken, misleading, duplicated,
or provenance-unknown files should be quarantined or removed rather than silently
kept.

## Commercial titles

For titles such as Chip Huyen's *AI Engineering*, use the official O'Reilly page,
O'Reilly Learning, a legitimate library, or a purchased copy. The author's official
companion repository may be kept locally because it is intentionally public, but it
is not the complete book.
